GNz7q

"OG" Little Red Dot － Bridghing Galaxies and Quasars at Cosmic Dawn

GNz7q is a remarkable object discovered at redshift z = 7.189, thought to represent a rare transition phase between compact starburst galaxies and rapidly growing quasars. Its dual nature makes it a unique laboratory for studying early black hole growth and galaxy evolution.

The object exhibits strong dust emission and broad-line features, suggesting both intense star formation and active black hole accretion. GNz7q was uncovered through deep HST imaging and further characterized by ALMA observations, showcasing the power of multi-wavelength and gravitational lensing techniques.

This discovery provides key insights into how black holes and galaxies co-evolve in the early Universe, marking a significant step forward in our understanding of cosmic history.
